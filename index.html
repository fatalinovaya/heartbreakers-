Editor's Letter

Every February we open this house down to its basement, and every February people arrive as themselves and leave as someone they only imagined.

Inside: seven films screened in silence, the faces that walked our carpet, the clothes they were reborn in, and the golden heart we handed to the ones who broke the room open.

Read it the way you'd wear a mask — as a beautiful way to disappear. Be our valentine.

— The Hosts

Contrabounds, on dressing the ball

How do you design for people with no faces?

“You dress the silhouette and the sound — the click of latex, the drag of lace. The body becomes the sentence. Every mask at that table was cut like couture and worn like armour.”

And the heart mask?

“A valentine you can't take off until dawn. That's the whole idea of the ball — you become the gift.”
